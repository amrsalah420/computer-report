Early computers were built to perform a series of single tasks, like a calculator. Basic operating system features were developed in the 1950s, and
their complex and more modern form didn't exit until the early 1960s. Hardware features were added that enabled use of runtime libraries, interrupts and parallel
processing. In 1980s, personal computers became popular and operating systems were made for them similar in concept to those used on larger computers.

In the 1940s, the earliest elecronic digital systems had no operating system. Electronic systems of this time were programmed on rows of mechanical
switches or by jumper wires on plugboards. These were special-purpose systems that generated ballistics tables for military or control the printing of payroll
checks from data on punched cards.
